**Action:** Prototype new preset ideas for plato-torch training rooms.

**Analysis:** From reading `fleet-knowledge/docs/DEADBAND-PROTOCOL.md`, the key is to design presets that enforce the P0→P1→P2 priority order. The `DeadbandRoom` preset likely already exists. New presets should explore variations: different environment complexities, stochastic rocks, multi-agent coordination, or partial observability.

**Prototype Idea 1: "MirrorPlayRoom"**
- Two agents in identical mazes.
- Each agent sees only its own maze.
- They must communicate via a limited channel to share rock maps (P0) and safe channels (P1) before optimizing (P2).
- Training goal: learn to transmit negative space efficiently.

**Prototype Idea 2: "GhostTileRoom"**
- Agents inherit a partial rock map from previous failed agents (ghost tiles).
- They must validate and expand the P0 map before proceeding.
- Tests trust in prior knowledge versus direct exploration.

**Prototype Idea 3: "ConstraintPropagationRoom"**
- Rocks are not static; they propagate constraints (e.g., adjacent cells become unsafe after some steps).
- Agents must continuously update P0 and P1 maps.
- Simulates dynamic environments like network failures or shifting API limits.
